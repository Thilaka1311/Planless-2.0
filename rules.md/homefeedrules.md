# HOME FEED RULES

1. The Home Feed is the primary discovery and participation surface of Planless.

2. The Home Feed should display:
- real-world plans
- social activity
- active participation
- friend-group coordination

3. The Home Feed must use:
- full-screen vertical plan cards
- one plan per viewport
- vertical snap scrolling
- swipe-first navigation

4. Only one plan should be visible at a time.

5. The next plan must not be visible until the user intentionally swipes.

6. The feed interaction system should behave similarly to:
- TikTok
- Instagram Reels
- YouTube Shorts

while being adapted specifically for:
- real-world plans
- social coordination
- friend-group discovery

7. The Home Feed should feel:
- immersive
- socially alive
- active
- lightweight
- emotionally real

8. Every swipe should feel like:
“discovering another real-world opportunity.”

9. Every plan card should prioritize information in the following order:
1. Activity
2. People / attendees
3. Timing
4. Venue
5. Secondary metadata

10. The activity or experience should remain the dominant visual focus of the screen.

11. The Home Feed should subtly communicate social activity using:
- attendee face piles
- participant counts
- active circles
- lightweight friend indicators
- recurring group familiarity

12. Social proof should remain lightweight and human-centered.

13. Avoid:
- follower systems
- creator hierarchies
- popularity rankings
- public scoreboards
- engagement farming systems

14. Plan cards must avoid:
- excessive metadata
- analytics-heavy layouts
- crowded action systems
- excessive badges
- large information clusters

15. Feed interactions must feel:
- thumb-friendly
- frictionless
- responsive
- native-feeling
- mobile-first

16. Motion should:
- improve clarity
- improve fluidity
- improve interaction confidence

17. Avoid:
- cinematic transitions
- over-animation
- distracting motion systems
- excessive bounce effects

18. The feed itself is the hero surface.

19. Navigation should remain visually secondary and minimally intrusive.

20. The Home Feed must avoid:
- nested scrolling systems
- complex filter dashboards
- overloaded category tabs
- multi-column layouts
- enterprise-style discovery systems

21. The feed should naturally support:
- spontaneous plans
- recurring plans
- scheduled social plans
- cultural/event-based plans
- friend-group activities

22. The Home Feed should optimize for:
- speed
- instant readability
- fast participation
- low cognitive load
- smooth scrolling performance

23. During MVP, the Home Feed should remain:
- simple
- fast
- intuitive
- socially active

over:
- feature-rich
- highly personalized
- algorithmically complex

24. The Home Feed should feel like:
“a live stream of real-world plans happening around your social world.”
